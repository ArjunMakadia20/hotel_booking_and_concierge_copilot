## 4. Strongest cancellation patterns
- **`deposit_type`** is the single most striking driver: **Non-Refund deposits cancel
  99.4%** of the time vs 28.4% for No-Deposit and 22.2% for Refundable. (See
  `cancellation_rate_by_deposit_type.png`.) This is a known quirk of this dataset.
- **`lead_time`**: cancellation rate rises monotonically with how far ahead the
  booking is made â€” bookings 365+ days out cancel far more than same-week bookings
  (`cancellation_rate_by_lead_time_group.png`, `cancellation_rate_by_lead_time.png`).
- **`total_of_special_requests`** and **`required_car_parking_spaces`**: more
  requests / needing parking â†’ **lower** cancellation (engaged, committed guests).
- **`previous_cancellations`**: guests who cancelled before cancel again far more.
- **`market_segment` / `distribution_channel`**: Groups and certain OTA/TA channels
  cancel more than Direct/Corporate.
- **`hotel`**: City Hotel cancels more than Resort Hotel.

## 6. Outliers (IQR, reported â€” not removed)
Checked the 13 key numeric fields (`outlier_report.csv`, `outlier_boxplots.png`).
Highest flagged rates: `adults` (24.9%), `booking_changes` (15.1%), `children`
(7.2%), `required_car_parking_spaces` (6.2%), `previous_cancellations` (5.4%),
`adr` (3.2%). **Most of these are legitimate rare-but-valid values** (e.g. large
parties, many booking changes, one extreme `adr`), not data errors. We **keep them**:
tree-based models are robust to outliers, and these tails carry real cancellation
signal. Only impossible values were corrected (negative `adr` â†’ 0).

## 7. Linear or non-linear?
